## Description

Jacob Andreas (Associate Professor, MIT EECS / CSAIL) leads a group studying the computational foundations of efficient language learning and building general-purpose systems that communicate with and learn from humans. A significant line of work involves understanding ML models — including techniques for interpreting models using language and analyzing the algorithms and inductive biases inside language models. His group has produced mechanistic/interpretability-adjacent work on what representations and computations LLMs implement.
