## Description

Sarah Wiegreffe (tenure-track Assistant Professor, UMD CS; member of the CLIP lab, affiliated with UMIACS and UMD's AI Interdisciplinary Institute) publicly announced she is recruiting 2 PhD students to work on language-model interpretability starting Fall 2026. Her research is at the intersection of NLP, empirical ML, and explainable AI, focused on the interpretability of LMs and neural networks to increase transparency, reliability, safety, and utility, including faithful natural-language explanations of models' internal operations. Recent and ongoing work includes the Mechanistic Interpretability Benchmark (MIB, ICML 2025), steering-vector circuits for long-form generation, interpreting latent reasoning models, and chain-of-thought monitoring (supported by a Coefficient Giving grant).

## Requirements

PhD applicants apply through the UMD CS PhD program and should mention her name/interest. Standard CS PhD prerequisites apply (strong ML/NLP background; research experience is a plus). Exact GRE/English-test/deadline requirements not stated on her page — verify on the UMD CS PhD admissions page.

## Why it fits

Direct match to mechanistic interpretability of LLMs. Wiegreffe explicitly works on mechanistic interpretability (co-author of the MIB benchmark and a BlackBoxNLP position paper on mechanistic interpretability), circuit-level analysis, steering vectors, and interpreting reasoning models — exactly the area requested. She is one of very few US faculty with an explicit, current (Nov 2025) public statement of recruiting specifically for LM interpretability in Fall 2026.
