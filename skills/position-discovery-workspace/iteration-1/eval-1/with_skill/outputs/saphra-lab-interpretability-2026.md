## Description

Naomi Saphra (currently Research Fellow at the Kempner Institute, Harvard; incoming Assistant Professor in Boston University's Faculty of Computing & Data Sciences) states on her homepage: "I am recruiting PhD students to begin in 2026 at Boston University." Her research aims to comprehensively understand language-model training, combining linguistics, optimization, learning dynamics, science of deep learning, interpretability, and behavioral analysis. Current goals include using training trajectories and between-run variation to identify which concepts are significant/distinct to a model, predicting model behavior by understanding models computationally and algorithmically, and using interpretability to understand the world (collaborations in science-of-discovery models).

## Requirements

Apply to BU's PhD program (CS / Computing & Data Sciences); you do not need to email her to apply. Strong fit with her published work expected. She is explicitly more interested in understanding systems than building them — applicants whose main goal is building/fixing models are a poor fit. No GRE/English/deadline details on her page — verify with BU.

## Why it fits

Interpretability of language models is a core pillar of her agenda, with a distinctive angle on training dynamics and the science of deep learning (how concepts/structure emerge during training). Good fit for an applicant interested in mechanistic/interpretability questions framed scientifically rather than as model engineering.
