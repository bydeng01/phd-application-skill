## Description

Aaron Mueller (Assistant Professor of CS, and by courtesy Data Science, at Boston University) runs a lab on NLP, computational linguistics, interpretability, and evaluation. A primary direction is causal and mechanistic interpretability: understanding and editing the causal mechanisms underlying language-model behaviors and generalization. He co-authored foundational interpretability work including "Sparse Feature Circuits," "Function Vectors in Large Language Models," and "The Quest for the Right Mediator: A History, Survey, and Theoretical Grounding of Causal Interpretability." His prospective-students page describes recruiting PhD students for the lab.

## Requirements

Apply to the Boston University Computer Science PhD program and mention his name; he reviews all applications that mention him. Do NOT email application materials. Desired background: prior ML/NLP research experience (ideally a first-authored paper in an ML/NLP venue) and familiarity with ML toolkits/methods including PyTorch. Linguistics/cognitive-science expertise is valued alongside ML.

## Why it fits

Among the most direct matches: his lab explicitly lists "causal and mechanistic interpretability methods and frameworks" as a core research direction, with a track record of circuit-level and causal-mediation work on LLMs (sparse feature circuits, function vectors, causal interpretability survey). Strong fit for mechanistic interpretability of LLMs.
