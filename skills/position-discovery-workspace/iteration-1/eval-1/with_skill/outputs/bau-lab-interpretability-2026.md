## Description

David Bau (Assistant Professor of CS, Khoury College, Northeastern University) leads the Bau Lab, which studies the structure and interpretation of deep networks and aims to be a leading lab for interpretable neural networks. His prospective-students page lists active areas of interest: AI alignment (measuring/ensuring transparency, honesty, understandability of huge models), AI knowledge extraction (deriving new human knowledge from high-performing AI, e.g. scientific or code LLMs), and deep-network programming tools to crack open, customize, and control models with hundreds of billions of parameters. He also leads the NSF National Deep Inference Fabric (NDIF) project, infrastructure for interpretability research on large models.

## Requirements

PhD applicants apply to the Northeastern Khoury CS PhD program and mention interest in his lab. He notes he cannot reply to inquiries, but to highlight an application you may email him with "Prospective Student" in the subject line and attach a CV plus links to papers representing your research interests. Standard Khoury PhD prerequisites apply; verify GRE/English/deadline on the Khoury admissions page.

## Why it fits

One of the most prominent academic mechanistic-interpretability labs in the US. Bau's work (causal tracing, model editing/ROME, function vectors, sparse feature circuits with collaborators) is central to mechanistic interpretability of LLMs, and the lab explicitly recruits students for interpretability/alignment. Excellent fit, and the NDIF infrastructure gives students direct access for large-model interpretability experiments.
