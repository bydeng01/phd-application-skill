# Mechanistic Interpretability of LLMs — US PhD Openings to Target

Research date: 2026-06-12

This is a list of US-based labs and professors who work on mechanistic interpretability (MI) of large language models and who recruit PhD students. For US CS PhD programs, you generally apply to the *department* (not the professor) by a December deadline, then name the faculty you want to work with. Always re-check each lab's "prospective students" page before applying, since recruiting status changes year to year.

---

## Tier 1 — Core mechanistic interpretability labs (strongest targets)

### David Bau — Northeastern University (Khoury College of Computer Sciences)
- One of the central academic MI labs ("Bau Lab"); pioneered deep-network interpretability and model editing. Leads/co-leads the NDIF (National Deep Inference Fabric) infrastructure project.
- Lab interests: AI alignment (transparency, honesty, understandability of large models), AI knowledge extraction, and deep-network programming tools for billion-parameter models.
- How to apply: Apply to the Northeastern Khoury PhD program and mention his lab. Optionally email davidbau@northeastern.edu with "Prospective Student" in the subject line, attaching a CV and links to representative papers. (He notes he cannot reply to all inquiries.)
- Student info page: https://baulab.info/students.html
- Apply: https://www.khoury.northeastern.edu/apply/phd-apply/

### Aaron Mueller — Boston University (CS, and by courtesy Data Science)
- Works squarely on causal and mechanistic interpretability, plus linguistically/cognitively informed evaluation and sample-efficient LMs. Co-author of the major MI survey "The Quest for the Right Mediator." Keynoted the 2025 New England Mechanistic Interpretability (NEMI) workshop.
- How to apply: Apply to the BU Computer Science PhD program by **December 15** and mention his name. He explicitly asks applicants NOT to email materials; he reaches out for interviews if he sees a fit. (Note: his prospective-students page currently still reads "Fall 2025" — confirm the current cycle before applying.)
- Prospective students page: https://aaronmueller.github.io/prospective.html
- Lab site: https://aaronmueller.github.io/

### Sarah Wiegreffe — University of Maryland (CS / UMIACS)
- Joined UMD as Assistant Professor in August 2025; studies interpretability and transparency of LMs and faithful natural-language explanations of model internals.
- Recruiting status: Reported to be **recruiting PhD students for Fall 2026**. Mention her in your UMD CS PhD application.
- Faculty page: https://www.umiacs.umd.edu/our-experts/faculty/sarah-wiegreffe
- Personal site: https://sarahwie.github.io/
- UMD profile: https://www.cs.umd.edu/people/sarahwie

## Tier 2 — Strong interpretability-adjacent labs at top departments

### Jacob Andreas — MIT (EECS / CSAIL)
- Associate Professor; research on the computational foundations of language learning, with substantial interpretability work on the internal representations of LMs.
- How to apply: Apply to the MIT EECS PhD program; see his contact page and advising statement before reaching out.
- Site: https://www.mit.edu/~jda/  | Research: http://jacobandreas.net/research/

### Christopher Potts — Stanford University (Linguistics; by courtesy CS; Stanford NLP Group)
- Professor and Chair of Linguistics. Long track record in interpretability / explainable AI and causal abstraction methods (collaborator on much of the "causal interpretability" line of work). Strong fit if you want a linguistics + NLP framing.
- How to apply: Apply via the relevant Stanford PhD program (Linguistics or CS) and connect with the Stanford NLP Group. Stanford HAI also runs a postdoc fellowship co-supervised by Potts.
- Profile: https://profiles.stanford.edu/christopher-potts  | Group: https://nlp.stanford.edu/

## How to find more / verify

- **New England Mechanistic Interpretability (NEMI) Workshop** — good map of active academic + industry MI researchers in the region: https://nemiconf.github.io/summer25/
- **ICML 2026 Mechanistic Interpretability Workshop** (organized by Neel Nanda et al.) — scan organizers and accepted-paper author affiliations for faculty who recruit.
- Annual application timing: most US CS PhD deadlines fall in **early-to-mid December** for the following fall. Confirm each department's deadline and each professor's recruiting status on their own site.

## Caveats / notes
- Several prominent MI people are currently in **industry, not academia**, so they do not directly take PhD students: Neel Nanda (Google DeepMind), Atticus Geiger (Goodfire), Eric Wallace (OpenAI), Been Kim (Google). They mentor through programs like MATS rather than advising PhDs — useful for research experience but not a PhD application target.
- Deadlines and "recruiting this cycle" claims above are based on web sources as of June 2026 and can change. Re-verify on the official department/faculty pages before applying. No deadlines were invented here; where a specific date is given (BU Dec 15; UMD Fall 2026 cycle) it comes from the lab's own page or department reporting.
