## Description

The EPSRC Centre for Doctoral Training (CDT) in Machine Learning Systems at the
University of Edinburgh's School of Informatics trains cohorts of ~12 PhD students
across the full ML-systems stack. It is funded by EPSRC to recruit five intakes
between 2024 and 2028, and applications for **September 2027 entry are confirmed to
open in October 2026**. Supervisors span Informatics, Engineering and Mathematics,
which includes researchers working on graph and geometric machine learning. The
applicant would pick/shape a project with a supervisor in the cohort, so a graph
neural network / geometric deep learning thesis is feasible if matched to the right
supervisor.

## Requirements

- Strong undergraduate/master's degree in a relevant quantitative/CS field.
- All applicants are eligible for the fully-funded UKRI studentship regardless of
  citizenship or fee status, provided they meet the standard PhD entry requirements.
- English-language requirements per University of Edinburgh PGR norms (verify exact test/score).

## Why it fits

Matches all of the applicant's hard filters: UK location, **2027 start**, and
**fully funded** (UKRI studentship covering fees + stipend; Edinburgh covers the
Home/International fee difference for international CDT applicants). The CDT's
breadth across Informatics/Maths/Engineering supervisors makes a graph neural
network or geometric deep learning project realistic, which is the applicant's core
interest. This is the cleanest match found for a 2027-start funded UK route.

## Notes

- Admission model: program/CDT cohort admission (apply to the CDT, funding is
