## Description

The EPSRC CDT in Autonomous Intelligent Machines and Systems (AIMS) at the University
of Oxford is a research-based doctoral programme combining machine learning, robotics,
vision, control and cyber-physical systems, with academic training and industry-led
projects. Its training explicitly includes **visual geometry, deep learning,
perception, generative models, segmentation and robot learning**. The 2026-27 cycle
is closed; **applications for 2027-28 entry open later in 2026** (registration for an
"applications open" alert is available).

## Requirements

- Strong degree in a relevant quantitative/engineering/CS field.
- Standard Oxford PGR entry and English-language requirements (verify on the AIMS/Oxford site).
- Funded studentships; confirm eligibility terms (Home vs international quotas) on the CDT page.

## Why it fits

UK location and **2027-28 entry**, **funded**. The CDT's emphasis on visual geometry
and geometric/representation learning within vision and robotics is adjacent to the
applicant's geometric deep learning interest, and graph-structured methods appear in
several supervisors' work. A plausible funded 2027 route if the applicant leans toward
geometric DL applied to vision/robotics rather than pure GNN theory.
