## Description

The ELLIS PhD Program is a pan-European, co-supervised doctoral program: each student
is co-advised by two ELLIS fellows/units based in different European countries, with a
mandatory exchange of at least 6 months at the international advisor's lab. ELLIS runs
a dedicated **Geometric Deep Learning research program** (and a related Graphs/relational
learning community), so applicants can target supervisors working directly on graph
neural networks and geometric deep learning across the network's European units
(e.g. Cambridge, and units in DE/NL/CH/FR/etc.). ELLIS issues an annual call for
applications; the call for the 2026 cycle (for a following-year start) has run, and a
new annual call would cover a 2027 start.

## Requirements

- Strong master's/undergraduate record in ML/CS/maths or related.
- Apply through the central ELLIS PhD application against chosen ELLIS supervisors;
  you typically list multiple potential advisors.
- Funding is provided by the host advisor(s)/unit, not centrally by ELLIS — so the
  "fully funded" status depends on the specific advisor's funded position.

## Why it fits

Directly on-topic: ELLIS has an explicit Geometric Deep Learning program and a
strong concentration of GNN / geometric DL researchers across UK and European units,
matching the applicant's field exactly. Pan-European scope satisfies the "UK or
Europe" filter. Co-supervision and the European mobility model are attractive for a
methods-focused PhD.
