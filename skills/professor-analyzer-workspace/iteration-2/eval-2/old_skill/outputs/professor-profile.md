## Research focus

Fei-Fei Li is one of the most prominent figures in modern AI, best known as the creator of
**ImageNet** and the ImageNet Challenge that helped catalyze the deep-learning era. Her
research center of gravity is **computer vision** and, increasingly, **"spatial
intelligence"** — building models that perceive, reason about, generate, and interact with
the 3D physical world. Her current threads include **embodied AI and robotics** (vision-
language-action models, robotic manipulation, embodied reasoning) and **world models** for
3D scene understanding and generation.

She is the inaugural Sequoia Professor in Computer Science at Stanford, founding co-director
of the Stanford Human-Centered AI Institute (HAI), former director of the Stanford AI Lab
(SAIL, 2013–2018), and runs the Stanford Vision and Learning Lab. In 2024 she co-founded and
serves as **CEO of World Labs**, a "spatial intelligence" startup (reported ~$1B+ valuation,
later reported around $1.25B) building frontier 3D world models. This dual role is material
for a prospective student (see Fit and Gaps).

**Important:** None of her primary research areas is natural language processing,
multilingual LLMs, or low-resource languages. Language appears in her work only insofar as
it is paired with vision/action (e.g. multimodal or vision-language models), not as
text-only NLP, and not with any multilingual or low-resource-language emphasis.

## Recent publications

Recent work associated with her lab and collaborators (2024–2026) clusters around embodied
AI, robotics, and spatial/visual reasoning — not text NLP. Representative recent threads
(titles surfaced via search; treat exact authorship as needing confirmation on her Scholar
page before citing in an email):

- **Embodied AI: From LLMs to World Models** (arXiv 2509.20021, 2025) — positions world
  models as the next step beyond language models for agents that act in the physical world.
  Takeaway: her framing explicitly contrasts language intelligence with *spatial*
  intelligence as the next frontier.
- **BEAR: Benchmarking and Enhancing Multimodal Language Models for Atomic Embodied
  Capabilities** (arXiv 2510.08759, 2025) — benchmark for embodied capabilities of
  multimodal LLMs. Takeaway: language is used as a multimodal interface to embodiment, not
  as a standalone NLP problem.
- **Mind to Hand: Purposeful Robotic Control via Embodied Reasoning** (arXiv 2512.08580) —
  reasoning-to-action for robotic control.
- **MosaicThinker: On-Device Visual Spatial Reasoning for Embodied AI** (arXiv 2602.07082) —
  spatial reasoning representations for embodied agents.
- Ongoing **BEHAVIOR** benchmark line (household robotics / embodied agents) from SVL.

She also published a widely circulated 2025 essay/manifesto defining "world models" and
spatial intelligence as the next decade's AI agenda, and was named a **Queen Elizabeth Prize
for Engineering** laureate (2025) and **VinFuture Prize** recipient (2024).

(I did not find any 2024–2026 publication by her in multilingual LLMs, low-resource-language
NLP, machine translation, or text-only language modeling.)

## Research agenda & open problems

The thread she is pulling on is clear and consistent: **moving AI "from seeing to doing"** —
from passive visual recognition toward agents with spatial intelligence that can perceive,
reason about, generate, and act in 3D environments. Likely next directions: better 3D world
models, generative 3D environments, embodied/robotic agents grounded in spatial reasoning,
and benchmarks for embodied capability. These are her stated open problems.

None of these open problems is about NLP, multilinguality, or low-resource languages.
