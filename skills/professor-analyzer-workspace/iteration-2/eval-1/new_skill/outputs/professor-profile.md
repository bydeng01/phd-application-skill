## Research focus

The Doudna Lab studies the structure, mechanism, and application of CRISPR-Cas RNA-guided
systems. Four stated research areas on the lab site: **Structure and Mechanism**, **New
Editing Tools**, **Diagnostics**, and **Editing in Cells**. A major and growing thrust is
**therapeutic genome editing** — getting editors into the right cells/tissues safely and
efficiently, both ex vivo and in vivo in mammals. This includes lipid-nanoparticle (LNP) and
engineered-particle delivery of Cas ribonucleoproteins (RNPs), enzyme engineering for faster/
safer editing, and reducing immunogenicity. The lab is tightly linked to the Innovative
Genomics Institute (IGI), which Doudna founded and which pushes toward affordable genetic
medicines.

## Recent publications (last ~3 yrs, with takeaways)

Delivery / therapeutics thread (most relevant to applicant):
- **Lung and liver editing by lipid nanoparticle delivery of a stable CRISPR-Cas9
  ribonucleoprotein** — Chen K, Han H, Zhao S, … Murthy N, Doudna JA. *Nat Biotechnol* 2024
  (PMID 39415058; bioRxiv 2023, PMID 38014175). *Takeaway:* a stabilized Cas9 RNP packaged in
  LNPs achieves organ-level (lung, liver) editing — directly an LNP-delivery-of-CRISPR result.
- **Packaged delivery of CRISPR-Cas9 ribonucleoproteins accelerates genome editing** — Karp H,
  Zoltek M, … Schepartz A, Doudna JA. *Nucleic Acids Res* 2025 (PMID 40036508). *Takeaway:*
  packaging RNPs (vs. naked protein) speeds and improves editing — delivery-vehicle engineering.
- **Mechanism-guided engineering of a minimal biological particle for genome editing** — Ngo W,
  Peukes J, … Nogales E, Doudna JA. *PNAS* 2025 (PMID 39793042). *Takeaway:* rational design of
  a minimal protein-based delivery particle ("eVLP"-style) for editor cargo.
- **Engineering self-deliverable ribonucleoproteins for genome editing in the brain** — Chen K,
  … Doudna JA. bioRxiv 2023 (PMID 38014180); and **Genome editing in the mouse brain with
  minimally immunogenic Cas9 RNPs** — Stahl EC, … Doudna JA. *Mol Ther* 2023 (PMID 37403358).
  *Takeaway:* tissue-targeted delivery + lowering RNP immunogenicity — a recurring therapeutic
  concern that overlaps the applicant's delivery interest.
- **A roadmap for affordable genetic medicines** — Kliegman M, … Urnov FD, Doudna JA. *Nature*
  2024 (PMID 39019069). *Takeaway:* the lab/IGI's stated direction toward scalable, affordable
  in vivo therapeutics — signals where the program is heading.

Enzyme engineering / mechanism thread:
- **Rapid DNA unwinding accelerates genome editing by engineered CRISPR-Cas9** — Eggers AR, …
  Doudna JA. *Cell* 2024 (PMID 38781968). Engineering Cas9 kinetics for better editing.
- **Rapid two-step target capture ensures efficient CRISPR-Cas9-guided genome editing** — Shi H,
  … Doudna JA. *Mol Cell* 2025 (PMID 40273916). Mechanism of target recognition.
- **Structure-guided discovery of ancestral CRISPR-Cas13 ribonucleases** — Yoon PH, … Doudna JA.
  *Science* 2024 (PMID 39024377). Discovery/engineering of new editor families.

Tools / diagnostics / ML thread:
- **Single-molecule live-cell RNA imaging with CRISPR-Csm** / **A 'CRISPR' way to visualize RNA
  in live cells** — Xia C, Colognori D, … Doudna JA. *Nat Biotechnol* 2025 (PMID 39071319 /
  s41587-025-02560-9). CRISPR-Csm for RNA imaging.
- **RNA language models predict mutations that improve RNA function** — Shulgina Y, … Doudna JA,
  Cate JHD. *Nat Commun* 2024 (PMID 39638800); **Functional protein mining with conformal
  guarantees** — Boger RS, … Jordan MI, Doudna JA. *Nat Commun* 2025 (PMID 39747192). A growing
  ML-for-biomolecules direction.

## Research agenda & open problems

Reading across the recent papers, the lab is pulling hard on: (1) **getting editors into
specific tissues in vivo** via LNPs and engineered minimal particles, with safety/immunogenicity
as the gating problem; (2) **engineering the editor itself** (faster unwinding, smaller enzymes,
new Cas families) to make delivery and precision easier; (3) **affordable, scalable genetic
medicines** as the translational north star (via IGI). Open problems the applicant could plausibly
touch: tissue/cell-type targeting of LNPs beyond liver, RNP stability and immunogenicity,
and formulation–payload co-design (matching a delivery vehicle to a given engineered editor).
