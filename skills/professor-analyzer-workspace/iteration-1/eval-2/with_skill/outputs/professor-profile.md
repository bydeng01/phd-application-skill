## Research focus

Fei-Fei Li is one of the founders of modern computer vision (she created ImageNet, which
catalyzed the deep-learning era). Her current research center of gravity is **spatial
intelligence**: getting machines to perceive, reason about, generate, and act in the 3D
physical world. This spans three connected threads:

- **Embodied AI and robot manipulation** — teaching agents to act in the 3D world, e.g.
  driving a robotic arm to perform everyday tasks from language instructions.
- **3D world models / generative spatial AI** — models that understand and generate
  semantically and physically coherent 3D environments. This is the explicit mission of
  **World Labs**, the startup she co-founded in early 2024 and now leads as CEO.
- **Human-centered AI** — she co-directs the Stanford Institute for Human-Centered AI
  (HAI), shaping AI policy and human-centered applications (including healthcare).

Importantly, her framing positions spatial intelligence as the *successor* to today's
language-centric LLMs. In World Labs' own words, world models' capabilities of reasoning
about complex worlds are "far beyond the reach of today's LLMs." Her work is essentially
*not* in the NLP / language-modeling tradition.

## Recent publications

(Grounded in search results; representative recent work, not an exhaustive list. Several
items below are co-authored with her students/collaborators in SVL.)

- **BEHAVIOR-1K** (CoRL 2023, ongoing benchmark line) — a human-centered embodied-AI
  benchmark of 1,000 everyday household activities in realistic simulation. Takeaway:
  pushes embodied agents toward broad, real-world household competence rather than narrow
  tasks.
- **VoxPoser** (2023) — composable 3D value maps that let an LLM compose robot-manipulation
  trajectories. Takeaway: uses a language model as a *planner over 3D space*, not as a
  language artifact in itself.
- **ReKep** (2024) — relational keypoint constraints for spatio-temporal reasoning in
  robotic manipulation. Takeaway: structured 3D/geometric reasoning for manipulation.
- **World Labs / Marble** (2025) — large world models that generate navigable 3D worlds
  from an image or text prompt (Marble is World Labs' first commercial product). Takeaway:
  her flagship current direction — generative spatial intelligence.

Recent recognition: VinFuture Prize (2024), Queen Elizabeth Prize for Engineering (2025),
TIME Person of the Year (2025).

## Research agenda & open problems

The trajectory points squarely at spatial intelligence: building foundation models that
understand, generate, and interact with 3D worlds, and embodied agents that act in them.
Open problems she is pulling on include 3D scene/world generation, physically and
geometrically consistent world models, and language-conditioned robot control. The near-term
direction is almost certainly more World Labs–style generative world models and embodied
manipulation — not language modeling.

A practical caveat: she now leads World Labs as CEO, which raises the question of how much
new on-campus PhD student supervision she is personally doing versus her SVL co-PIs and the
HAI orbit.
